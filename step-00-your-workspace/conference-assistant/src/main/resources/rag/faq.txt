JavaZone 2026 Attendee FAQ

Q: Where is the conference?
A: Oslo Spektrum, Sonja Henies plass 2, 0185 Oslo, Norway.

Q: What are the registration desk hours?
A: The registration desk is open from 07:30 on Day 1 (September 9) and from 08:00 on Day 2 (September 10). It closes each day at 17:00. Please pick up your badge on arrival.

Q: What is the wifi password?
A: Network: JavaZone2026, Password: JavaRocks! The network is available throughout Oslo Spektrum including all session rooms, the expo hall, and the lounge areas.

Q: What food and drinks are available?
A: A full breakfast buffet is served from 08:00. Lunch is provided from 12:00 to 13:00 in the main expo hall. Afternoon snacks and coffee are available from 15:00. All meals are included in the conference ticket. Please note any dietary requirements at registration. Vegan, vegetarian, gluten-free, and halal options are available.

Q: Is there a code of conduct?
A: Yes. JavaZone is committed to providing a harassment-free experience for everyone. All attendees, speakers, sponsors, and staff are required to follow the code of conduct. It is available at javazone.no/conduct. Violations can be reported to the conference team at conduct@javazone.no or in person at the registration desk.

Q: How do I get from the airport to the venue?
A: Take the Airport Express Train (Flytoget) from Oslo Airport (Gardermoen) to Oslo Central Station. The journey takes approximately 20 minutes. Oslo Spektrum is a 5-minute walk from the station.

Q: Where can I find the conference schedule?
A: The full schedule is in the conference app (available on iOS and Android) and at program.javazone.no. Printed schedules are available at the registration desk.

Q: Can I attend any session I want?
A: Yes, sessions are first-come first-served. Rooms fill up quickly for popular talks. Workshops in Room 1 and Room 2 may have limited capacity and require advance registration in the app.

Q: Is there a lost and found?
A: Yes, at the registration desk. The team will hold found items until the end of Day 2.

Q: What are the quiet hours?
A: The lounge on level 2 is designated as a quiet area throughout the conference for those who need a break from the busy floor.
